How You Can Help

Create your own event

Find a Location

The first step in creating a clean up event is finding the proper location to hold the event. This can be in areas such as parking lots, school campuses, and side streets. In order to properly ascertain the optimal location, it could be beneficial to ask members of your community where they believe the most affected areas are.

Reach Out

Once you have found the perfect location, reach out to any governing bodies and make sure that you receive consent to clean up in the respective area. This is essential in order to avoid any legal compilations when the event does take place.

Obtain Materials

The third step to a great clean up event is finding materials for your volunteers such as trash bags, safety vests, and trash grabbers. These materials can be obtained from your town, county, or other clean up organizations in and around your area.

Logistical Analysis

The fourth step in planning a clean up event is mapping out your clean up location, avoiding high-traffic areas, organizing people into different groups, and sectioning off certain areas in order to compartmentalize the event.

Find Volunteers

The most crucial step in creating a clean up event is finding volunteers that will help you clean up your community. This step can also be one of the most challenging aspects to a clean up event and a few ways you can obtain volunteers is by reaching out to people at your school, job, or social circles to help out.

Conduct Event

The final step in creating a clean up event is executing the event. Make sure to reach out to your volunteers and let them know of any extraneous conditions that could arise such as the weather, arrange a meeting, a spot for your volunteers, and always have a positive attitude during the event. Lastly, make sure to have fun and get your volunteers excited about making a difference.
